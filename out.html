Isle of May

 
    The Isle of May is an island in the Forth Estuary

    which is inhabited by 120,000 puffins

    from April to August. There’s a ferry that runs

    from Anstruther which allows you to spend

    several hours exploring the island.

V&A Dundee 

 

    Only 30 minutes away by bus is the newly-opened

    V&A museum, Dundee’s “living room”.

    An offshoot of the V&A in London, this museum

    showcases the best of Scottish design.

    Entry to the permanent exhibition is free and the cafe

    and shop are also well worth a visit.

Anstruther Fish & Chips 

 

  If you find yourself in Anstruther

  after walking along the coastal path,

  or after a trip to the Isle of May,

  you have to stop at the Anstruther Fish & Chip shop

  to try some of the best fish and chips in the UK!

  Either sit in, or eat your fish and chips on the harbour pier.
